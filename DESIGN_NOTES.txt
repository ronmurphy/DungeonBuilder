DUNGEON BUILDER — ECONOMY & GAMEPLAY DESIGN
=============================================

CORE LOOP: Build dungeon -> Adventurers explore -> Get rated -> Earn gold -> Expand

STARTUP:
- Simple title screen: 3 save slots + New Game
- No difficulty tiers — everyone gets the same big map and 2,000 gold
- Currency = gold

WEEKLY ADVENTURER VISIT:
- Every week, a party of adventurers "arrives" (simulated, not physical pathfinding)
- They rate the dungeon and loot items

LOOT CHANCES (weighted per item type):
  Coin              100%  — always taken
  Chest              80%  — obvious treasure target
  Trophy             70%  — shiny, valuable
  Weapon (sword/spear) 50% — useful gear
  Shield (round/rect)  50% — useful gear
  Weapon Rack        40%  — bulky but worth it
  Barrel             25%  — might have loot inside
  Banner             15%  — decorative, low priority
  Statue             10%  — too heavy to carry easily
  Column/Damaged Col  5%  — who steals a column? but someone will
  Wood Structure/Sup  5%  — structural, not interesting
  Trap                0%  — they avoid/trigger them, don't take
  Characters          0%  — NPCs, not lootable
  Stairs              0%  — can't steal stairs
  Tree                0%  — it's a tree

PAYOUT: Looted items pay back 100% of their placement cost.

RATING FORMULA (1-5 stars):
  Size score:       1 point per 10 placed tiles (floors + walls + items)
  Variety score:    5 points per unique item type used
  Danger score:    10 points per trap, 8 points per monster (character)
  Treasure score:   6 points per chest, 3 points per coin, 4 points per weapon/shield
  Atmosphere score:  3 points per banner/column/statue/barrel, 2 points per wood structure

STAR THRESHOLDS:
  1 star  =   0 - 49  points (boring dungeon)
  2 stars =  50 - 99  (decent crawl)
  3 stars = 100 - 199 (good dungeon)
  4 stars = 200 - 349 (great dungeon)
  5 stars = 350+      (legendary)

GOLD PAYOUT PER STAR:
  1 star  =  50 gold
  2 stars = 125 gold
  3 stars = 250 gold
  4 stars = 400 gold
  5 stars = 600 gold
  (Plus loot payout on top)

ECONOMY BALANCE NOTES:
- 2000 gold start makes ~2 decent rooms + a few items = 1 star early on
- 5 star / 600 gold is minimum break-even for large dungeons
- Traps + monsters = long-term investment (never looted, high rating points)
- Chests/coins = high rating but get stolen, need restocking

FUTURE BONUS SYSTEM (not yet coded):
- Characters as "employees" that attract more/better adventurers
- Emote-yes / emote-no animations for bonus reactions
- NPCs bring in extra business, pushing gold past 600g ceiling
- More adventurer party types (some prefer traps, some prefer treasure)
- Room detection (enclosed walls = bonus points)
- Dungeon reputation (rolling average of last N ratings)
